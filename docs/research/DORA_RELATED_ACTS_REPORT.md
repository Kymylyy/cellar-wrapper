  OPTIONAL { ?work cdm:resource_legal_in-force ?in_force }
  OPTIONAL {
    ?expr cdm:expression_belongs_to_work ?work .
    ?expr cdm:expression_uses_language <http://publications.europa.eu/resource/authority/language/ENG> .

## Notes

1. **RTS vs. plain Delegated Regulations**: Not all delegated regulations under DORA are technically "RTS". Specifically, (EU) 2024/1502 (criteria for critical ICT TPPs) and (EU) 2024/1505 (oversight fees) are delegated regulations that supplement DORA but were not drafted by the ESAs as RTS. The remaining delegated regulations contain RTS drafted by EBA, ESMA, and/or EIOPA.

2. **ESA involvement**: The RTS under DORA are developed jointly by the three European Supervisory Authorities (EBA, ESMA, EIOPA) through the Joint Committee of the ESAs, reflecting DORA's cross-sectoral scope.

3. **Article basis**: The article references in the table above are inferred from the official titles. The CELLAR metadata does not provide article-level granularity in its `resource_legal_based_on_resource_legal` predicate.

4. **No formal amendments to DORA**: As of the query date, DORA itself has not been formally amended by any adopted legislation. The only proposal that would amend DORA is the Financial Data Access (FIDA) proposal (COM(2023) 360).

5. **9 corrigenda**: DORA has been corrected nine times since its original publication, which is an unusually high number and may reflect the complexity and breadth of the regulation.

6. **Consolidated texts of amended regulations**: DORA amends five other EU regulations (CRA, EMIR, MiFIR, CSDR, Benchmarks Regulation), and consolidated versions of those regulations (reflecting DORA's amendments, effective from 17 January 2025) are also linked in CELLAR.
